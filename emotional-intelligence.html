Exploring EQ and it's importance in the work place and in life plus it's differences from IQ. EQ can have a significant determination on all aspects of your life. It greatly affects the way you relate to yourself and others. The core curriculum develops your self-knowledge through working on key emotional intelligence skills. This will benefit both your learning journey and your employability.

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Google definition - The capacity to be aware of, control, and express one's emotions, and to handle interpersonal relationships judiciously and empathetically. "emotional intelligence is the key to both personal and professional success"

How is EQ different to IQ?

IQ is an score from an intelligence test, IQ is believed to be something you are born, itâ€™s important to note that IQ and knowledge are different. IQ is measured as a ability to think locicaly, mathematically and problem solve. The IQ you get is measured against people your age so high IQ would mean your above average in your age group.

Just like IQ, EQ is a score from a test but from a emotional test, where an IQ would test logic an EQ test will measure your ability to recognize emotion in yourself and in others and be able to use that information to guide your decisions. It is a level of understanding of how you react to emotions and being aware and in control in a positive manner.

A scene from the TV show young sheldon covers the differences Sheldon is known to have a incredibly high IQ and his sister not so much although running a different type of test shows his sisters strengths. Check it out.

Why is EQ important?

A high EQ allows you to build relationships, defuse conflict, decrease the stress levels of a team and boost morale and improve job satisfaction. EQ is a leading factor in many successful peoples lives almost more important that IQ. Having both a high IQ and EQ would lead to a very competent life and a good career.
